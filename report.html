Assessment Overview

This targeted waste audit of Erskine Park Shopping Centre identifies significant inefficiencies in current waste services, infrastructure deficiencies, and opportunities for service optimization and cost reduction.

The audit revealed service overcapacity, stream contamination, and inadequate infrastructure resulting in elevated operating costs and operational risks.

Through waste stream separation, infrastructure upgrades, and bin service optimization, the site could achieve net savings of approximately $10,204 annually while preparing for incoming EPA regulatory requirements.

Issues & Risks

Critical Issues Identified

Unsecured Infrastructure

Bins located in open-air public areas subject to dumping, rainwater ingress, and vehicle access issues. Creates operational and financial risks.

Weather Contamination

Lack of enclosure leads to rainwater ingress, causing overweight loads, collection failures, and additional fees.

Stream Contamination

General Waste includes food waste, recyclables, and empty oil containers that should be diverted to appropriate streams.

Service Inconsistency

Missed pickups due to obstructed bin access and unclear scheduling with service provider.

Infrastructure Assessment

Critical Infrastructure Deficiencies

  • • No weather protection: Bins exposed to rain, causing weight issues and collection failures
  • • Public access: Unauthorized dumping increases costs and contamination
  • • Vehicle access problems: Obstructed collection routes cause missed services
  • • Aged oil collection: Rusted, waterlogged drum creates health and safety risks

Financial Impact of Current Issues

  • • Overweight charges from rainwater contamination
  • • Excessive collection frequency due to over-servicing
  • • Additional costs from contaminated materials going to landfill
  • • Service failure compensation and management time

Recommendations

A. Bin Optimization Strategy

Current Configuration

  • • 2 × 3m³ General Waste bins (daily collection)
  • • 2 × 3m³ Cardboard bins (daily collection)
  • • Under-utilized capacity
  • • Weather-damaged contents

Proposed Configuration

  • • 1 × 4.5m³ General Waste bin (4x/week collection)
  • • 1 × 4.5m³ Cardboard bin (3x/week collection)
  • • Better capacity utilization
  • • Weather-protected environment

Rationale: Bin weights suggest consolidation is viable without operational risk once coupled with new stream implementation and infrastructure protection.
