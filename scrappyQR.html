How It Works

THE LOOP.

Scrappy is an autonomous robotic system designed to recover eligible containers in high-traffic public environments. Built to work alongside existing F&B, facilities management and cleaning operations teams, Scrappy slots into real-world workflows without disruption.

The system is being validated across two deployment phases at QPAC — one of Australia's premier performing arts venues, seeking to prove that robotics can meaningfully improve recycling outcomes at scale.

Each phase is pushing the boundary of what autonomous recycling robots can achieve in high traffic public spaces. The learnings from each iteration directly informing the next, improving mechanical handling, enclosure design and operational picking speed.

Scrappy isn't just a robot. It's a platform for rethinking how we close the loop on waste in public areas.

01
Phase 1 · Baseline
Research & Validation

Initial feasibility study establishing the baseline opportunity for container recovery at QPAC. Identifying eligible container volumes, operational constraints and integration requirements with site teams.

  • Container volume audit & opportunity sizing
  • Operational workflow mapping
  • Prototype development & bench testing
02
Phase 2 · Live Deployment
Operational at QPAC

Scrappy is deployed into a live environment for the first time, operating during large events alongside venue teams. Over 1,005 containers have been recovered in this trial so far.

  • Real-world handling, positioning & speed assessment
  • Enclosure design & serviceability refinement
  • Performance under fluctuating waste volumes
03
Phase 3 · Next
Engineering Iteration

Live deployment confirmed the opportunity and surfaced clear areas for improvement. The next phase focuses on speed, robot performance and enclosure optimisation, all engineered for higher accuracy and throughput.

  • Improved mechanical arm geometry
  • Faster cycle times & throughput
  • Expanded venue deployment
∞
The Vision
Public spaces at Scale

Scrappy is designed to scale. The goal is a network of autonomous recycling robots operating across stadiums, arts venues, and large public spaces, making circular economy outcomes the default, not the exception.

  • Multi-venue rollout capability
  • Integrated container deposit scheme
  • Real-time recycling analytics dashboard

The Technology

ROBOT AT
WORK.

01

Computer Vision

An overhead camera continuously scans the waste stream, using a custom-trained AI model to detect and classify eligible containers, aluminium cans and plastic bottles, in real time.

02

AI Decision Making

The AI calculates each container's position, orientation and retrieval priority, scoring picks based on accessibility and recycling value before issuing a command to the robot arm.

03

Robotic Retrieval

A 6-axis robotic arm executes each pick with sub-centimetre precision, extracting containers from general waste and depositing them into the recycling stream, fully autonomously, no human required.

About

BUILT THROUGH
PARTNERSHIP.

Scrappy is an independent R&D project bringing together robotics engineers, venue operations specialists and sustainability advocates. We believe the path to a circular economy runs through the places people actually gather and that automation is the key to making recycling effortless at scale.

Our approach is hands-on, iterative, and firmly rooted in real-world deployment. We don't test in labs, we test in high traffic public spaces, with real teams, during real events.

The collaboration and hands-on support from the QPAC team has been instrumental in making this ongoing R&D possible. Their involvement is helping us refine a system that operates effectively in real environments while delivering stronger recycling and sustainability outcomes.

Coca-Cola
In partnership with Coca-Cola, Scrappy is helping close the loop on one of the world's most recognised beverage containers, turning post-event waste into recovered resource.
QPAC
Queensland Performing Arts Centre is providing the venue environment for Phases 1 and 2, working closely with the broader Scrappy technical team to integrate robotics into day-to-day operations.
Shrunk
Shrunk Innovation Group built and engineered Scrappy, delivering the embedded firmware, robotics hardware, and IoT systems that power the platform. Based in Melbourne, Shrunk specialises in end-to-end IoT, computer vision, and hardware solutions across all areas of sustainability.
